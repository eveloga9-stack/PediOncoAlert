Sepsis Pocket Card

Night Curriculum — First 60 Minutes
Antibiotics in ≤ 60 min  |  Fluids in ≤ 30 min of order
Recognize Early Shock
  • Tachycardia — earliest compensatory sign
  • Delayed cap refill (>2 sec), cool/mottled extremities
  • Altered mental status — lethargy, weak cry, irritability
  • Hypotension is a LATE sign — do not wait for it
  • SpO₂ may be normal despite poor perfusion
Initial Management
  • Assess perfusion, apply O₂ via NC/face mask
  • 20 mL/kg LR/NS bolus via rapid infuser (10 mL/kg if cardiac hx)
  • Draw labs: VBG, lactate, CBC, CMP, CRP, blood cultures (central + peripheral)
  • Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics within 30 min of order
  • Administer Tylenol for fever
  • Reassess perfusion & BP after each bolus
  • Notify provider/attending
Antibiotic Quick Reference
Scenario Antibiotic
Neutropenic or unstable Cefepime
Not neutropenic + stable Ceftriaxone
Add Vancomycin if: Hypotension, line/catheter infection, cellulitis, pneumonia, severe mucositis, chills/rigors with catheter flush, suspected meningitis
Escalate to Meropenem if: Septic shock needing ICU, rapid deterioration despite fluids + cefepime
Consider Amikacin if: Chills/rigors during catheter flush, evidence of sepsis, known MDR organism colonization
Escalation Triggers
  • No improvement after 40–60 mL/kg fluids
  • Persistent hypotension or worsening perfusion
  • Altered mental status
  • Increasing O₂ requirement
  • Stop fluids if: worsening tachycardia, gallop, rales, hypoxia (fluid overload)
Stress-dose hydrocortisone: 2 mg/kg IV or 100 mg/m2 (max 100 mg) — especially if on chronic steroids or adrenal suppression
High-Risk Patients
  • Leukemia on induction
  • AML patients
  • Solid tumors 7–10 days post-chemo (expected nadir)
  • Relapsed/refractory cases
  • Post-transplant or chronic immunosuppression
